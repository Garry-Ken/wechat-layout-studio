# Content Scoring Rubric

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. A publishable acquisition piece should usually reach 7.5+ composite and have no dimension below 6.

## Dimensions

- Topic sharpness: The piece names a specific problem, audience, and judgment.
- Knowledge activation: It uses private notes, cases, methods, or customer observations that generic web content would not have.
- Information gain: The reader learns something non-obvious or gets a better mental model.
- Proof value: Claims are supported by examples, data, experience, or concrete scenarios.
- Usefulness: The reader can act after reading or watching.
- Spreadability: The idea has a memorable phrase, tension, novelty, or identity signal worth sharing.
- Conversion fit: The CTA naturally connects to the user's business without feeling bolted on.
- Narrative energy: The structure keeps momentum and avoids textbook exposition.

## Composite

Use this weighting for knowledge-worker acquisition content:

- Virality = topic sharpness 20%, information gain 25%, spreadability 35%, narrative energy 20%.
- Conversion = knowledge activation 25%, proof value 20%, usefulness 25%, conversion fit 30%.
- Composite = virality 45%, conversion 55%.

## Prediction Log

Before publishing, write:

- Main reader: who should feel "this is about me".
- Share trigger: why someone would forward it.
- Conversion trigger: what pain makes them contact the author.
- Risk: what may feel too generic, too salesy, or too hard to believe.
- T+7d review: compare predicted shares, saves, comments, inquiries, and actual results; then update the rubric.
